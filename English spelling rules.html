English Spelling Guide

Mastering Tricky Patterns, Suffixes, & Rules 📖

1. The Magic "E" Rule (Silent E)

When a short word ends with a consonant and then the letter e, the silent e makes the first vowel say its own name (turning it into a long vowel sound).

Hop → Hope (The girl hopes it will be sunny tomorrow.)
Bit → Bite (The puppy took a small bite of her treats.)
Cut → Cute (The fluffy cat looks incredibly cute sleeping there.)

2. Dropping the Silent "E"

When adding a suffix (like -ing or -ed), check if the ending starts with a vowel or a consonant:

Rule A: Drop "E" if suffix starts with a VOWEL

Make + ing → Making: She is making a wonderful new layout for her website.
Love + able → Lovable: That small kitten is completely lovable.

Rule B: Keep "E" if suffix starts with a CONSONANT

Care + ful → Careful: The girl was very careful while carrying the glass bowl.
Excite + ment → Excitement: The puppies barked with pure excitement.

3. The 1-1-1 Doubling Rule

Double the final consonant if a short word has 1 syllable, 1 short vowel, and ends in 1 consonant before adding a vowel suffix:

  • Run + ing → Running: Look at how fast that puppy is running across the grass!
  • Stop + ed → Stopped: The girl stopped cycling to let a duck cross the road safely.
  • Shop + er → Shopper: The smart shopper bought a beautiful collar for her cat.

4. Changing "Y" to "I"

Look at the letter right before the y to know what to do:

Pattern Action Example
Consonant + Y Change Y to I Happy + ly → Happily (The girl smiled happily.)
Vowel + Y Keep the Y Play + ed → Played (The kitten played with yarn.)

*Exception: Keep the "y" if adding -ing (e.g., try → trying) so we don't double the letter i!

5. The Classic "I before E" Rhyme

"I before E, except after C, or when sounded like 'A' as in neighbor and weigh!"
Standard (I before E): She values her friend very highly.
After a C (E before I): The girl will receive a package containing her new books.
Sounds like "A" (E before I): Her cat has soft, beige fur.
